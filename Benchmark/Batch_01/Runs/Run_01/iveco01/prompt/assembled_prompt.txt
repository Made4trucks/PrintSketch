================ PRINTSKETCH MISSION ================

# MISSION

PrintSketch does not create truck illustrations.

PrintSketch recreates the customer's own truck as a premium piece of printable wall art.

Every generated artwork must preserve the identity of the real vehicle shown in the uploaded photo.

The owner should immediately recognize their own truck.

The final artwork is not a photograph.

The final artwork is not an AI illustration.

The final artwork is a professionally designed product created specifically for FDM 3D printing.

Every design decision must support one or more of these goals:

- preserve truck identity
- improve printability
- create a premium appearance
- simplify without losing character

If a design decision does not support these goals,

it should not be made.

================ DESIGN RULES =======================

GENERAL STYLE

Create a premium black-and-white industrial wall decoration designed specifically for FDM 3D printing.

The final artwork must look like a finished decorative product, not a photograph.

Use only solid black and white.

No grayscale.

No gradients.

No shadows.

No textures.

No realistic rendering.

No sketch effect.

White space is an intentional part of the design.

The final artwork should feel clean, elegant and premium.

The artwork should resemble premium laser-cut industrial wall art rather than a black illustration.

---

# STYLE DNA

The artwork should feel light, elegant and premium.

White space is an intentional design element.

Approximately 70–80% of the artwork should remain white.

Use black only where it improves recognition of the truck.

Avoid large solid black areas.

The cabin should remain visually clean.

The windshield should stay mostly white.

Use smooth flowing curves.

Create strong visual hierarchy.

The truck should immediately attract the viewer's attention.

The artwork should resemble premium laser-cut industrial wall art.

The final design should look expensive.

The final design should look balanced.

The final design should be suitable for display in an office, garage or home.

Every element should contribute to a premium industrial appearance.

---

## Visual Hierarchy

The viewer must recognize the truck within the first second.

Create a clear visual hierarchy.

Priority order:

1. Cabin silhouette
2. Grille
3. Headlights
4. Windshield
5. Owner-specific accessories
6. Company name
7. Secondary details

If necessary, simplify lower-priority details to preserve higher-priority elements.

The viewer's eye should always be drawn to the cabin first.

Large visual shapes are more important than small decorative details.

The overall silhouette of the truck must always remain immediately recognizable.

# DECISION PRIORITY

When two design goals conflict, always follow this priority order.

1. Preserve truck identity.

2. Preserve printability.

3. Preserve overall composition.

4. Preserve readability.

5. Preserve premium appearance.

6. Preserve secondary decorative details.

If a lower-priority element prevents a higher-priority goal,
simplify or remove the lower-priority element.

Never sacrifice truck identity for decorative complexity.

Never sacrifice printability for photographic accuracy.

Always optimize for the final physical product.

DECISION WORKFLOW

Before preserving or simplifying any element, follow this workflow.

1. Identify the element.

2. Determine whether it is part of the truck identity.

3. Determine whether it is physically printable using a 0.4 mm FDM nozzle.

4. If the element is too small:
   - enlarge it if it is part of the truck identity,
   - simplify it if its shape is important,
   - remove it only if it has no influence on the truck identity.

5. Never add elements that are not present in the original photograph.

6. Never change the number or position of visible lights, accessories or owner-specific details.

7. Ensure that the entire truck including all preserved elements remains inside the circular frame with a safe margin.

================ COMPOSITION RULES ==================

# COMPOSITION

Place the truck inside a balanced circular composition.

The truck should remain the main visual element.

Preserve the original framing and natural proportions of the vehicle.

If the trailer is removed, extend the rear chassis only enough to preserve the visual balance of the truck.

Never allow the remaining chassis or trailer section to occupy more than approximately 20% of the total truck length inside the composition.

## Perspective

Preserve the original viewing angle.

Never rotate the truck.

Never convert a front three-quarter view into a side view.

Never exaggerate perspective.

Never change the apparent camera height.

Preserve the natural proportions created by the original camera position.

================ IDENTITY RULES =====================

TRUCK IDENTITY

The generated artwork must represent the exact truck shown in the uploaded photo.

Never transform the truck into another model or another brand.

Preserve the characteristic cabin shape.

Preserve the identity of the grille.

Preserve the original grille geometry, including the exact number, spacing and proportions of horizontal grille bars whenever they are clearly visible.

Preserve the identity of the headlights.

Preserve the mirrors.

Preserve the wheels whenever visible.

Preserve the roof accessories.

Preserve the sun visor.

Preserve owner-installed accessories whenever possible.

Company decals should remain recognizable.

Brand name should remain readable.

Model designation should remain readable.

License plate should remain recognizable whenever possible.

Never invent text.

Never replace text with different words.

If text cannot be reproduced accurately, simplify it without changing its meaning.

Never add new company logos, stickers or decorations.

When simplifying the truck, preserve the elements that make this specific truck unique.

The owner should immediately recognize their own vehicle.

Preserve all unique truck identity elements, including sun visor lettering, company names, custom banners, model badges, auxiliary lights, grille lights, roof lights and other owner-specific visual features whenever they are clearly visible in the original photo.

---

DECISION HIERARCHY

When simplifying the artwork, always preserve elements in the following order of importance.

LEVEL 1 — Truck Identity

Never remove or significantly simplify:

- truck brand
- truck model
- cabin shape
- grille
- headlights
- mirrors

LEVEL 2 — Company Identity

Preserve whenever visible:

- company name
- company logo
- company graphics
- visor branding

LEVEL 3 — Owner Signatures

Preserve whenever they remain printable:

- personalized windshield names
- truck model designation
- registration plate
- custom curtains
- owner-installed accessories
- unique decorations

LEVEL 4 — Technical Noise

Remove or simplify whenever they reduce printability:

- inspection stickers
- certification labels
- warning decals
- tiny technical markings
- serial labels
- other visually insignificant details

================ PRINTABILITY RULES =================

# PRINT RULES

## General

The artwork is designed specifically for FDM 3D printing.

Reference printer: Bambu Lab P1S.

Reference nozzle: 0.4 mm.

Design for the physical object first, not for the digital preview.

The final artwork should be immediately ready for slicing.

Prioritize structural strength, readability and clean printable geometry over photographic accuracy.

---

## Line Quality

Minimum printable line width: 0.6 mm.

Maintain consistent line thickness throughout the design.

Use bold, continuous lines.

Avoid extremely thin lines.

Every line should have a structural purpose.

---

## Structural Integrity

Avoid floating elements.

Avoid isolated islands.

Avoid fragile connections.

Every important detail must connect naturally to the main design.

The final artwork should print cleanly without manual editing.

---

## Small Details

If a detail is too small to print reliably, simplify it while preserving the character of the truck.

Simplify very small details before removing important visual identity.

Prefer fewer strong shapes instead of many tiny details.

Never create unreadable micro-details.

---

## Text

Only preserve text that contributes to the identity of the truck.

If small decorative text cannot be reproduced with reliable readability at the target print size, simplify or remove it instead of creating unreadable tiny details.

---

## Round Lights

Round lights and auxiliary light units must be simplified into bold printable geometry.

Do not reproduce:

- internal reflector patterns
- concentric rings
- thin spokes
- star patterns
- micro-details inside the lamp

Preserve only the outer shape and visual identity of the light.

---

## Multiple Auxiliary Lights

If multiple identical auxiliary lights are mounted together, preserve the overall arrangement and spacing.

Do not reproduce every internal reflector detail.

Each light should remain simple, bold and printable.

---

## Meshes & Protective Grilles

Large mesh patterns, protective guards and grille inserts should preserve their overall appearance but be simplified into bold printable geometry.

Avoid tiny openings and dense mesh structures.

---

## Final Validation

Before accepting the artwork ask:

Would this successfully print with a standard 0.4 mm nozzle?

If not,

simplify.

Reduce tiny holes, thin rings and micro details that cannot be reproduced reliably with a 0.4 mm nozzle.

The wheel should immediately look correct, even if some internal details are simplified.

Prioritize visual identity over mechanical accuracy.
