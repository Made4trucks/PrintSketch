# Component Simplification

Every component should be simplified independently.

Do not apply the same simplification strategy to every object.

Each component has different importance for truck identity and different manufacturing requirements.

---

## Grille

The grille defines the truck.

Preserve its overall shape and characteristic geometry.

Do NOT reproduce every internal mesh or every thin bar.

Instead:

- preserve the overall pattern
- preserve proportions
- merge tiny elements into larger printable surfaces
- keep the grille recognizable from normal viewing distance

---

## Headlights

Headlights are identity elements.

However:

Never reproduce every internal reflector.

Never reproduce microscopic optics.

Instead:

- preserve the outer silhouette
- preserve the characteristic light signature
- simplify all internal geometry

The result should immediately identify the truck model without copying photographic complexity.

---

## Wheels

Wheels are secondary identity elements.

Prefer:

- simple rim geometry
- larger openings
- strong outer contour

Avoid:

- tiny spokes
- decorative holes
- miniature bolts

---

## Mirrors

Preserve:

- overall size
- overall position

Ignore:

- mounting brackets
- tiny supports

---

## Windshield Text

Readable identity has priority.

If text is readable in the original:

increase its visual size by approximately 15–20%.

Do not reproduce decorative textures around text.

Keep only readable lettering.

---

## License Plates

License plates are important identity markers.

Prefer increasing readable character size by approximately 15–20%.

Keep spacing clean.

Do not reproduce microscopic borders or screws.

---

## Company Logos

Preserve:

- overall proportions
- recognizable silhouette

Simplify:

- internal decorative elements
- tiny shapes

Never redesign logos.

---

## Decorative Accessories

Keep only accessories that contribute to truck identity.

Ignore decorative details that become visual noise after printing.

---

## General Rule

Every simplification must answer one question:

"Will this improve the final printed object?"

If yes,

simplify.

If no,

preserve.
