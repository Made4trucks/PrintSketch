# Manufacturing Reasoning

The goal is not to reproduce a photograph.

The goal is to design a premium two-color artwork that will be physically manufactured using FDM 3D printing.

Always think like an industrial product designer.

Before preserving any detail, evaluate whether it will still be recognizable after manufacturing.

## Manufacturing Process

For every visible element, follow this reasoning:

1. Recognize the object.
2. Determine its importance for truck identity.
3. Estimate whether it can be manufactured cleanly.
4. Decide whether to:
   - Preserve
   - Slightly enlarge
   - Simplify
   - Merge with surrounding geometry
   - Omit
5. Validate that the result still represents the original truck.

Never copy photographic detail without evaluating manufacturability first.

## Manufacturing Constraints

Assume the artwork will be printed using:

- FDM technology
- 0.4 mm nozzle
- PLA
- Two-color print
- Finished diameter between 150 mm and 220 mm

Every design decision must respect these limitations.

## Readability First

Prefer:

- larger clean shapes
- connected geometry
- clear silhouettes
- readable identity
- balanced negative space

Avoid:

- microscopic details
- isolated islands
- fragile thin geometry
- decorative noise

## Functional Simplification

Simplification is not loss of quality.

Simplification is an intentional design decision that improves manufacturability while preserving identity.

Whenever two solutions are possible:

Choose the version that will produce the cleaner physical print.

## Manufacturing Confidence

Always ask:

"If this artwork were printed today,
would every important identity element still be immediately recognizable?"

If the answer is uncertain,

simplify further.
