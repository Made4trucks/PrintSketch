# Manufacturing Profile

## Mission

The generated artwork is not a digital illustration.

It is a premium physical wall decoration designed specifically for FDM 3D printing.

Every design decision must optimize the quality of the final manufactured product rather than maximize photographic accuracy.

---

# Target Manufacturing Environment

Printer:
Bambu Lab P1S

Printing Technology:
FDM

Material:
PLA

Nozzle Diameter:
0.4 mm

Layer Height:
0.20 mm

Final Product Diameter:
150–220 mm

Construction:
White circular base with a raised black top layer.

Viewing Distance:
Approximately 1–3 meters.

---

# Manufacturing Mindset

Before generating the artwork, imagine that the object already exists as a real printed product.

Every visual element must survive the manufacturing process and remain attractive after printing.

The final object should look like a professionally designed industrial product, not a converted photograph.

---

# Manufacturing Decision Process

Before preserving any detail, ask yourself:

Can this detail still be clearly recognized after being printed at only 150–220 mm?

Can it be manufactured reliably using a 0.4 mm nozzle?

Will it still improve the appearance of the final product when viewed from normal room distance?

If the answer is NO, simplify it.

Never preserve microscopic details simply because they exist in the photograph.

---

# Manufacturing Priorities

Always prioritize, in this order:

1. Strong overall truck silhouette.
2. Brand recognition.
3. Truck model recognition.
4. Company identity.
5. Characteristic accessories.
6. Readability.
7. Fine decorative details.

---

# Simplification Philosophy

Large recognizable shapes are always more valuable than many tiny details.

Whenever multiple tiny elements visually merge after printing, redesign them into a single clean printable element.

The customer should immediately recognize the truck from several meters away.

The goal is not photographic realism.

The goal is premium manufactured artwork.

---

# Final Rule

Think like an industrial product designer.

Not like a photographer.

Not like an illustrator.

Not like an SVG converter.

Always design for the final printed object.
