# Final Validation

Before generating the final artwork, perform one complete validation pass.

The objective is to verify that the artwork represents the original truck while being optimized for FDM manufacturing.

---

## Identity Validation

Confirm that:

- Manufacturer is correct.
- Truck model has not changed.
- Company names are preserved whenever readable.
- Owner identity is preserved.
- Decorative owner elements are preserved whenever important.
- No identity element has been invented.

---

## Manufacturing Validation

Confirm that:

- All important elements are printable.
- Geometry is connected whenever possible.
- Thin details have been simplified.
- Decorative noise has been removed.
- Negative space remains clean.
- Small isolated islands have been avoided.

---

## Readability Validation

Confirm that:

- Company names remain readable.
- Windshield text remains readable.
- License plate remains readable.
- Model badges remain readable.
- Important identity markings have sufficient visual size.

Readable identity has priority over photographic scale.

---

## Composition Validation

Confirm that:

- Truck remains fully inside the circular frame.
- Nothing important touches the border.
- Visual balance is maintained.
- The truck remains the dominant subject.

---

## Consistency Validation

Ask the following questions:

- Does this still represent the same truck?
- Would the owner immediately recognize it?
- Would another truck enthusiast recognize it?
- Does every simplification improve the final print?
- Has anything been invented?
- Has anything important been removed?

If any answer is uncertain,

revise the artwork before finalizing.

---

## Final Principle

Design the object that should be manufactured.

Not the photograph that was taken.
