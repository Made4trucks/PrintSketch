# PRINTSKETCH GOLDEN RULES
Version: 1.0

These rules define the philosophy of PrintSketch.

Every future prompt, engine update, application feature and design decision must follow these principles.

Breaking a Golden Rule requires a conscious decision and documented justification.

---

# 1. Identity Before Beauty

The truck must always be immediately recognizable as the customer's own vehicle.

Recognition is always more important than artistic interpretation.

---

# 2. Print Before Perfection

Optimize every artwork for the final physical 3D print, not for the digital preview.

If a detail looks good on screen but prints poorly, simplify it.

---

# 3. White Space Is Premium

White space is an active design element.

Do not fill empty areas without purpose.

A clean design always looks more premium.

---

# 4. Every Black Shape Must Have A Purpose

Black areas exist only to improve:

- recognition
- readability
- printability
- structural strength

Never use black only for decoration.

---

# 5. Preserve Character, Simplify Detail

When a detail cannot be reproduced reliably,
preserve the character instead of the exact geometry.

Never lose the identity of the truck.

---

# 6. Never Invent

Never invent:

- truck parts
- accessories
- logos
- company names
- text
- decorations

Everything must originate from the uploaded photo.

---

# 7. The Cabin Is The Hero

The cabin is always the main subject.

Everything else exists only to support it.

---

# 8. Readability Wins

Readable details are more valuable than perfectly copied but unreadable details.

If necessary:

simplify.

Never create unreadable micro-details.

---

# 9. Structural Printability

Every line should improve the physical object.

Avoid:

- floating islands
- fragile parts
- extremely thin details

The artwork must print successfully on a standard 0.4 mm nozzle.

---

# 10. Would You Proudly Sell It?

Before accepting the artwork ask:

Would I print this?

Would I proudly sell it?

Would I proudly hang it on my own wall?

If the answer is not YES,

the artwork is not finished.
