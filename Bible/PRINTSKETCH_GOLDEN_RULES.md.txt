# PRINTSKETCH GOLDEN RULES
Version: 1.0

These rules define the philosophy of PrintSketch.

Every future prompt, engine update, application feature and design decision must follow these principles.

Breaking a Golden Rule requires a conscious decision and documented justification.

---

# 1. Identity Before Beauty

The truck must always be immediately recognizable as the customer's own vehicle.

Recognition is always more important than artistic interpretation.

---

# 2. Print Before Perfection

Optimize every artwork for the final physical 3D print, not for the digital preview.

If a detail looks good on screen but prints poorly, simplify it.

---

# 3. White Space Is Premium

White space is an active design element.

Do not fill empty areas without purpose.

A clean design always looks more premium.

---

# 4. Every Black Shape Must Have A Purpose

Black areas exist only to improve:

- recognition
- readability
- printability
- structural strength

Never use black only for decoration.

---

# 5. Preserve Character, Simplify Detail

When a detail cannot be reproduced reliably,
preserve the character instead of the exact geometry.

Never lose the identity of the truck.

---

# 6. Never Invent

Never invent:

- truck parts
- accessories
- logos
- company names
- text
- decorations

Everything must originate from the uploaded photo.

---

# 7. The Cabin Is The Hero

The cabin is always the main subject.

Everything else exists only to support it.

---

# 8. Readability Wins

Readable details are more valuable than perfectly copied but unreadable details.

If necessary:

simplify.

Never create unreadable micro-details.

---

# 9. Structural Printability

Every line should improve the physical object.

Avoid:

- floating islands
- fragile parts
- extremely thin details

The artwork must print successfully on a standard 0.4 mm nozzle.

---

# 10. Would You Proudly Sell It?

Before accepting the artwork ask:

Would I print this?

Would I proudly sell it?

Would I proudly hang it on my own wall?

If the answer is not YES,

the artwork is not finished.

---

# Batch_01 Benchmark Rules


## Rule 1 — Intelligent Text Scaling

Small but important identity markings must remain readable after conversion to an FDM printable design.

Automatically increase the size of:

- model badges (e.g. 480, FH16, S500)
- license plates
- windshield names
- company names
- visor lettering

Slight scaling is preferred over preserving exact photographic proportions if it significantly improves readability and printability.


## Rule 2 — Headlight Simplification

Preserve the exact number, position and overall shape of all headlights and auxiliary lights.

Do not redesign the lighting layout.

Simplify only the internal details of each light so they remain printable with a 0.4 mm nozzle.

The visual identity of the vehicle must always take priority over decorative internal lamp details.


## Rule 3 — Grille Strategy

Preserve the unique identity of each manufacturer's grille.

Keep the characteristic proportions, bar layout and overall shape.

Simplify repetitive micro geometry whenever it cannot be reliably printed with a 0.4 mm nozzle.

The grille should always remain recognizable before it becomes fully accurate.


## Rule 4 — Wheel Simplification

Preserve the overall appearance and recognizable design of the wheels.

Maintain the number of wheels, rim proportions and characteristic style.

Reduce tiny holes, thin rings and micro details that cannot be reproduced reliably with a 0.4 mm nozzle.

The wheel should immediately look correct, even if some internal details are simplified.


## Rule 5 — Window Interior

The area behind windows should remain clean and visually open.

Avoid filling window interiors with unnecessary dark shapes.

Preserve only meaningful interior identity elements such as:

- curtains
- driver names
- mascots
- dashboard decorations
- interior accessories that contribute to the truck's identity

Everything else should be omitted to improve readability and overall visual balance.


## Rule 6 — Intelligent Detail Filtering

Not every visible detail from the original photo should be preserved.

Automatically remove or simplify elements that fall below the minimum printable size for a 0.4 mm nozzle, including:

- tiny stickers
- micro logos
- decorative symbols
- very small text
- thin ornamental lines

Prioritize recognizable identity over absolute photographic accuracy.

Removing insignificant details is preferred over producing fragile or unreadable geometry.


## Rule 7 — Standard vs Premium Detail Mode

Design every project primarily for reliable FDM printing with a 0.4 mm nozzle.

For standard production:

- simplify complex side graphics
- simplify wheel details
- simplify grille micro geometry
- simplify headlight internals
- remove decorative micro elements

For premium projects (0.2 mm nozzle), a higher level of detail may be preserved when it significantly improves the customer's final product.

The standard workflow should always prioritize reliability, printability and repeatable production.
